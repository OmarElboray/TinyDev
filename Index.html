How We Build

Opinions,
not defaults.

Three principles that govern every line we ship. Non-negotiable across every project.

Principle 01

Ship the smallest thing that works

Every feature is a liability until proven otherwise. We start from constraints, not capabilities — then remove anything the user doesn't reach for. A 12 KB extension beats a 2 MB one every time.

Principle 02

Own the full stack, or own nothing

Shallow abstractions create invisible failure modes. We write to the platform where we can, keep third-party surface area explicit and narrow, and don't depend on anything we can't read in an afternoon.

Principle 03

Defaults should be honest

Software that auto-opts users into tracking or engagement loops isn't neutral — it's a choice. We treat privacy as a load-bearing requirement, not a settings toggle added at the end.
